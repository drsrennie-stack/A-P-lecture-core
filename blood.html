Blood

Blood is the body's only liquid connective tissue: cells suspended in a fluid matrix. We cover plasma and its proteins, the formed elements (red cells, the five white cells, and platelets), where blood is made, and the disorders that follow when one component goes wrong. Dr. Sharilyn Rennie

What Blood Is Made Of

Part 1 · Overview

Blood, an overview

Cardiovascular
  • Plasma: the liquid matrix, about 55% of blood volume.
  • Formed elements: the cells and fragments, about 45%. The hematocrit is the percent that is red cells; the buffy coat is the thin layer of white cells and platelets in a spun sample.
  • Functions: transport (gases, nutrients, wastes, hormones), regulation (temperature, pH), and protection (clotting, immune defense).

Part 1 · Plasma

Plasma and its proteins

Cardiovascular

Plasma is about 92% water; its most abundant solutes are the plasma proteins.

Part 1 · Red cells

Erythrocytes, built for oxygen

Histology
  • Biconcave disc: thinner in the center, giving a large surface for gas exchange.
  • No nucleus and few organelles, leaving maximum room for hemoglobin, the iron protein that binds oxygen.
  • A flexible membrane lets it fold single-file through the narrowest capillaries.
  • Lifespan about 120 days, then recycled by the spleen and liver.

Part 2 · Platelets & formation

Platelets and hematopoiesis

Histology
  • Platelets are cell fragments (pinched off from megakaryocytes) that plug breaks and start clotting.
  • Hematopoiesis: all formed elements arise in the red bone marrow from a single type of stem cell, which divides down separate lines into red cells, white cells, and platelets.

Name the cell

Practice

Depth of Knowledge 1 · Recall

Which leukocyte is the most numerous and the first responder to a bacterial infection?

Answer

Neutrophil. It is the most common white cell and the first to engulf bacteria.

Part 3 · Skill / concept (DOK 2)

Apply the structure

Practice

Depth of Knowledge 2 · Skill / concept

Why does a mature red blood cell eject its nucleus and most organelles?

Answer

To hold more hemoglobin. Losing the nucleus and organelles maximizes oxygen-carrying capacity.

Part 3 · Strategic reasoning (DOK 3)

Reason it through

Practice

Depth of Knowledge 3 · Strategic reasoning

In sickle cell disease, why does a single abnormal hemoglobin cause capillary blockages and pain?

Answer

The cell becomes rigid and sickle-shaped. It loses the flexibility to squeeze through capillaries, so it lodges and blocks flow, causing pain crises.

Wrap-up

Key takeaways

Summary
  • Blood = plasma (55%) + formed elements (45%); it is a fluid connective tissue.
  • Plasma proteins: albumin (osmotic pressure), globulins (antibodies), fibrinogen (clotting).
  • Red cells are biconcave, anucleate, hemoglobin-packed; the five white cells defend; platelets clot.
  • All form in red bone marrow from stem cells.
  • Disorders track to a component: anemia/sickle cell/polycythemia (red), leukemia (white), thrombocytopenia (platelets), hemophilia (clotting).
