The Urinary System

The urinary system filters the blood and makes urine. The kidneys do the work, balancing water, ions, and pH and helping set blood pressure; the ureters, bladder, and urethra carry and store the urine. Dr. Sharilyn Rennie

Part 1 · Overview

The urinary system, an overview

Urinary
  • Kidneys filter blood and form urine; ureters carry urine to the bladder (storage); the urethra carries it out.
  • Kidney roles: filter wastes, balance water, ions, and pH, and help regulate blood pressure.

Part 1 · Gross anatomy

The kidney up close

Urinary
  • Retroperitoneal, bean-shaped, with three coverings (renal fascia, perirenal fat, fibrous capsule).
  • Interior: an outer cortex and an inner medulla of renal pyramids separated by columns; each pyramid drains at a papilla.
  • Urine collects in minor and major calyces, then the renal pelvis, exiting at the hilum into the ureter.

Part 1 · The nephron

Parts of a nephron

Urinary
  • Renal corpuscle: the glomerulus (capillary tuft) inside the glomerular (Bowman) capsule, where filtration begins.
  • Renal tubule: the proximal convoluted tubule, the nephron loop (descending and ascending limbs), and the distal convoluted tubule, draining into a collecting duct.
  • Two nephron types
    TypeFeatures
    Cortical (~85%)Short loops barely entering the medulla; peritubular capillaries
    Juxtamedullary (~15%)Long loops deep into the medulla, served by the vasa recta; they concentrate urine

Part 1 · JGA & filter

The JGA and the filtration membrane

Urinary
  • Juxtaglomerular apparatus: where the distal tubule touches its own arterioles. The macula densa senses sodium/chloride; the juxtaglomerular cells release renin when blood pressure is low.
  • The three-layer filtration membrane
    LayerHolds back
    Fenestrated endotheliumBlood cells (lets plasma pass)
    Basement membraneLarge proteins
    Podocyte filtration slitsRemaining small proteins

Part 2 · Path of urine

Bladder, urethra, and the path of urine

Urinary
  • The bladder's detrusor muscle contracts to void; the internal urethral sphincter is involuntary, the external is voluntary.
  • The male urethra is long (prostatic, membranous, spongy parts); the female urethra is short.
  • Path of urine: collecting duct -> minor and major calyces -> renal pelvis -> ureter -> bladder -> urethra.

Depth of Knowledge 1 · Recall

Where in the nephron does filtration of the blood begin?

Answer

The renal corpuscle. Blood is filtered from the glomerulus into the glomerular (Bowman) capsule.

Part 3 · Skill / concept (DOK 2)

Apply the anatomy

Practice

Depth of Knowledge 2 · Skill / concept

Why do juxtamedullary nephrons, with their long loops, matter for concentrating urine?

Answer

Their long loops reach the medulla. Going deep into the salty medulla lets them recover water and concentrate the urine.

Part 3 · Strategic reasoning (DOK 3)

Reason it through

Practice

Depth of Knowledge 3 · Strategic reasoning

A urinalysis shows protein and blood cells in the urine, which the filtration membrane should hold back. Which part has likely failed?

Answer

The filtration membrane (glomerulus). Normally it blocks cells and proteins; finding them in urine points to glomerular damage, as in glomerulonephritis.

Wrap-up

Key takeaways

Summary
  • System: kidneys, ureters, bladder, urethra; the kidneys filter and balance.
  • Kidney = cortex + medulla (pyramids), draining through calyces and the renal pelvis.
  • The nephron (corpuscle + tubule) is the working unit; juxtamedullary nephrons concentrate urine.
  • The filtration membrane is a three-layer sieve; the JGA releases renin to defend blood pressure.
  • Path of urine: collecting duct -> calyces -> pelvis -> ureter -> bladder -> urethra.
